If you live in or near to urban areas, there’s a good chance that you will have caught a glimpse of foxes that give you access to the world of a beautiful, caring, playful and intelligent animal.

Lots of my dormouse photos show torpid dormice because when they’re fully awake they’re usually too fast to photograph!

Red foxes are often portrayed as ‘bold’ and ‘fearless’ in the media, but actually they can be quite shy, skittish creatures, making photographing them a challenge.

I recommend getting down to bunny eye level they were suitably distracted by the clover so not too difficult to photograph.

Being able to predict sporadic movements–even to a small degree–can make the difference between getting and missing a photo, especially since squirrels are such fast creatures.

While getting an entire large animal in focus such as a deer is not always possible, getting the eye closest to you sharp is imperative!
